Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I had a patient come into the pharmacy with a prescription that had a list of items on it. After spending some time processing the prescription and getting the medication ready for the patient, they tell me that they do not have the money to pay for the medication. Although setting up accounts for patients to pay into at their own pace was not something we usually did, I felt like they could not go without their medication as they had recently been discharged from the hospital and they needed their medications especially the new ones. So, I made the decision to talk to my manager and see if we could set up an account so that the patient could take their time paying for the medications. I knew that their health was paramount and that if we told them that they had to pay or else they would not be able to get the medication, that they would probably just prefer to go without them. This way their health would deteriorate and the cycle would continue. Reflecting on the decision now, I do not regret it and I still feel proud of myself for trying my best to better that patient’s health outcomes.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I am from a very close-knit family and throughout my childhood, we had always been told to work hard, to be kind and to contribute to society in any way we could. Seeing my older siblings and older cousins practise these values when I was younger influenced my decision to pharmacy, and then now to delve into some software development. I look up to them and feel inspired by what they achieve to work hard myself and succeed in whatever it is that I need to do.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My strengths are that having been through quite a lot of challenging instances in my work and personal life, I have a higher level of maturity which I think enables me to compartmentalise my work and study life. Even when I get frustrated, I do not feel like I want to give up so easily but instead take a break and try again.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I have only ever studied health sciences and nothing else so in a way, my learning has been quite ‘narrow’ and I am a little bit worried that my mind would not be able to adapt to the ever-changing curriculum that we go through each sprint.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

Back at university, we would often have group projects that we had to work together on and it was always challenging to get the ball rolling whenever everyone in the group had a different personality or a different way of doing things. One strategy I did was to just take charge of the meeting and write everyone’s ideas down on a whiteboard so we could all talk about the best way to go about our project and to listen to what each other had to say. This seemed to be helpful as visually we could all see what our ideas were and also it made everyone feel like they were being heard and their ideas were important. Now, I would likely do the same but I would also maybe find a few examples of things that had been done before on a certain topic just so we had some examples to go through to get us started.
